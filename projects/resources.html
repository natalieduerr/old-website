about

One of the first projects I was assigned to at OHO Interactive was to create an Online Resources platform for a client on retainer, meaning they are a previous client that has hours with us each month to improve their site. With their time for the next few months they wanted us to create this resource platform. I was tasked with creating wireframes and the designs for the listing page and individual pages for the three types of resources: article, video, and infographic. All of the work I produced was overseen by Kelly O’Foran, Visual and UX Designer. The final site will be live soon and this page will be updated with a link as soon as it launches!

wireframing

Before I got down and dirty in wireframes I looked at many different blogs to see how they presented their content. The biggest challenge for this project was figuring out a way to display an infographic in an accessible way. Currently the client was just uploading a PDF for people to read, however that doesn’t work for people who use screen readers or with Google’s SEO. The best way to solve this problem was to pull as much text out, while still using the imagery from the infographic. In wireframing I developed two answers, one that would be the most time consuming, but provide the best results, and one that would be easier, solve the problems, but not be as visually appealing.

The images above showcase the same information, displayed in three different ways. The first is an example of how the infographic content could be easily uploaded, as an image with a caption and proper alt-text. The second image shows how the infographic could be split up even further, using a card component I created. In this way, a smaller image with the matching text and source information is pulled out. The last image showcases this card component on mobile, it becomes a slider that the user can move left/right to reveal the other cards.

Providing these two solutions made the client happy because it allowed them to create new, exciting content and also quickly update their old infographic posts. I included a button to download the PDF as well, so users can still access the infographic in full.

The other challenge with this project was creating the listing page. I wanted to give them the ability to highlight a single resource, a way to display resources that did and did not have an image, and a way to tell users which type of resource they would be seeing. The final wireframes for desktop and mobile for that listing can be seen below!

During the wireframe process I was able to account for several of the clients concerns. I was able to showcase resources that didn't have an image, show the type of resource, and the associated programs.

applying the brand

Since the client recently had a website overhaul, now was not the time to get creative with their brand. They already had several web design motifs that I was to incorporate. However, these rules really helped me do well on my first design project at OHO. Below are the final designs for listing and infographic pages.

For the client team, it was really important that not every resource need an image. In high fidelity screens, I was able to make the text based listing look intriguing. I created text-based tags to reinforce the three different resource types. All listings were designed to be visually accesible as well, with a black gradient helping to make the text more visible.
