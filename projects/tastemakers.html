about

Tastemakers Magazine is a Northeastern University based magazine that allows students to have a voice in the music industry. As a designer, we use a style guide to keep the basics consistent and work from there. The design team consists of about 12 students and Art Director(s) who work independently on their spreads. There are multiple design meetings each cycle to see each other's work and give/receive critique. The spreads are then combined and printed to create the bi-semester magazine. My spreads are presented from most recent to least recent.

issue 54

My article focused on how many artists now-a-days create personalities (even multiple) for the public. And since it featured some of my favorite artists currently, this spread was fun to work on. With the idea of split identities and concealing their true-selves, I went with splitting the CMYK colors to create illustrations with contrasting sharp, black text. The final spread can be seen below.

issue 53

For issue 53 I was tasked with designing the spread for an interview with Queen of Jeans who were the performers at Tastemakers Presents. My first instinct was to listen to their music to develop the color scheme for my spread. For me, their music was imbued with darks colors with pops or orange and pink. Thankfully, the photographer had the same vision and the spread pulled together very well.

issue 51

The second spread I worked on as part of Tastemakers was about music piracy. I decided to have a little fun with this one and go with a pirate theme. I incorporated the iconic pirate flag and a treasure chest with the most pirated albums of the year inside. I went with a blue background with a wave pattern to symbolize the water and waves of the ocean. Though piracy is a serious issue and topic, I was able to lighten the article and create visual intrigue through the theme,

issue 48

On my first issue with Tastemakers I worked on an article about Jazz near our campus. Titled "Columbus Ave. Culture Hub Turned Campus Corner", I couldn't help but incorporate a green street sign that many students see daily into the spread. By creating this graphic, I better helped place the article, as readers could recognize that this is located very close to them. With the article being about jazz, I chose a deep purple for the background and then bright pops of color for the accents. In order to incorporate the movement of Jazz I added semi-transparent versions of the illustration around in the background.
