Branding an annual expo hitting a new milestone

category
branding
print design
role
designer @ scout studio
date
october 2017 - november 2017
in collaboration with
cara jennings

discovery and iterating

As part of Scout, I worked with IDEA, Northeastern's student-led venture accelerator, to create awareness of their organization as well as create all material for this year's NEXPO. NEXPO is Northeastern's Entrepreneurship Expo, where ventures who have worked with IDEA are showcased. The event is for students, mentors, inventors, and anyone in the area who is interested, so it was important our designs be inclusive for all of them.

We first started at looking at what had been done before. This year's NEXPO was in a new location and bigger than ever before. IDEA asked that we make sure we did something new to relay that fact. Below are the initial ideas our team developed.

finalizing the brand

Of those four prototypes, the fourth one was the favorite. From there, I further developed the concept to be approved. In order to give the pattern more depth, we added the pattern again, at a lower opacity and in a different position. The colors of the arrows were changed as well based on the client's feedback. Below is our finished theme...

print materials

With a solidified brand, it was time to design materials to promote the event with. I was tasked with creating 4x4 table cards and 8.5x11 flyers. In order to make all the print materials feel connected, I developed type styles and ways to use the brand visual. The brand visual was always large and mostly off the page (not visible). By creating these rules, I was able to make sure other print materials done by other designers still fit within the system

The cards were used for tabling within the community and at various events. They were sized so they would be able to easily fit into someone's pocket. The flyers had three different taglines, to target different audience groups. These flyers were hung up around Northeastern and the nearby area, using whichever tagline fit that places area best. View a PDF of the flyers.

With a new and bigger location, it was important to help way find guests. With the help from the event planner from IDEA, we decided to create and place several signs at main points at campus. At key locations at campus, a sign would give a general reminder of where the location was and where to head. Signs with general arrows were created to put along the path from the Ruggles train station to ISEC, the route most guests would be taking.

The last big design push for this conference was the booklet. This booklet would be handed out to all attendees with the purpose of helping them learn more about the event, ventures presenting, and IDEA. Using the rules developed before and a strong grid, I was able to quickly lay out this booklet to accommodate IDEA's last-minute changes.

This 28-page booklet showcases NEXPO, the 20 ventures at the showcase, and the people and organizations who made it possible. A notes section was added to the end to allow the attendees to take notes as they walked around the expo space. View a PDF of the booklet.
